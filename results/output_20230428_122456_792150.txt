 The four main themes that appear more frequently are: 
1. The importance of breaking down concepts into microsteps: This theme was selected because it is important for teachers to break down concepts into smaller, more manageable parts in order to ensure that students are understanding the material. 
2. The use of frequent assessment: This theme was selected because it is important for teachers to constantly assess students in order to ensure that they are understanding the material. 
3. The use of bonus questions: This theme was selected because it is important for teachers to create bonus questions that engage students who have already mastered the material, while still allowing the teacher to provide individual help to those who need it. 
4. The need for abstract thinking: This theme was selected because it is important for teachers to provide guidance to students in order to help them understand abstract concepts.
